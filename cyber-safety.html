Golden Rules of the Internet

Be Kind Online

Treat others online the same way you would treat them in person. Use nice words and be respectful.

Keep Information Private

Never share your real name, address, phone number, or school name with strangers online.

Tell a Trusted Adult

If something online makes you feel scared, sad, or uncomfortable, tell a parent or teacher right away.

Balance Screen Time

Take breaks from screens to play outside, read books, and spend time with family and friends.

Ask Before You Click

Always ask a grown-up before downloading anything or visiting new websites.

Don't Meet Online Friends

People online might not be who they say they are. Never meet someone in person that you only know from the internet.

Creating Strong Passwords

Stop Cyberbullying

What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is when someone uses technology to be mean to others. This includes:

  • Sending mean messages or emails
  • Posting hurtful comments
  • Sharing embarrassing photos without permission
  • Leaving someone out of online groups on purpose

What to Do if You See Cyberbullying

1

Don't Join In

Never participate in cyberbullying, even if others are doing it.

2

Don't Forward

Don't share or forward mean messages or embarrassing content.

3

Support the Person

Be kind to the person being bullied. Send them a nice message.

4

Tell an Adult

Report cyberbullying to a parent, teacher, or other trusted adult.
