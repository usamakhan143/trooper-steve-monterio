Essential Water Safety Rules

Always Swim with a Grown-up

Never swim alone. Always have an adult watching who knows how to swim and can help if needed.

Wear Life Jackets

Use proper life jackets when boating or if you're learning to swim.

Use the Buddy System

Always swim with a friend and keep an eye on each other.

No Running on Pool Decks

Walk carefully around pools. Wet surfaces can be very slippery.

Know How to Call for Help

If someone needs help, get an adult immediately. Don't try to rescue them yourself.

Respect the Water

Water can be dangerous. Always follow the rules and listen to lifeguards.

Different Places, Different Rules

Pool Safety

Always enter feet first
Check water depth before jumping
Stay in shallow end if you can't swim well
Know where the pool ladder is
Don't swim during thunderstorms

Beach & Ocean Safety

Swim only where lifeguards are present
Watch out for waves and currents
Stay close to shore
Watch for sea creatures
Use sunscreen to protect your skin

What to Do in an Emergency

Learn the steps to help someone safely in a water emergency

1

Shout for Help

If you see someone in trouble, yell "HELP!" as loud as you can to get an adult's attention.

2

Get an Adult

Run to find a lifeguard, parent, or any adult who can help immediately.

3

Throw, Don't Go

If there's a life ring or pool noodle nearby, throw it to the person. Never jump in to rescue someone yourself.

4

Call 911 if Needed

If an adult tells you to, call 911. Know your address and explain what happened.

Know Your Swimming Level

Different swimming abilities require different safety precautions

Beginning Swimmer

You should:

  • Always wear a life jacket
  • Stay in shallow water
  • Have an adult within arm's reach
  • Practice floating and kicking

Learning Swimmer

You can:

  • Float on your back and front
  • Swim a short distance
  • Tread water for a few seconds
  • Jump in and get back to the wall

Strong Swimmer

You can:

  • Swim continuously for 25 yards
  • Tread water for 1 minute
  • Dive safely in deep water
  • Help others (with adult guidance)

Remember: Even strong swimmers need to follow safety rules!

No matter how well you swim, always have adult supervision and follow pool rules.
