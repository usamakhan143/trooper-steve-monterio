Car Safety Rules

Always Wear Your Seat Belt

Put on your seat belt as soon as you get in the car, before the car starts moving. It should fit snugly across your chest and lap.

Use the Right Car Seat

Children should use booster seats until they're big enough for the seat belt to fit properly (usually around 8-12 years old).

0-2 Rear-facing car seat
2-8 Booster seat
8+ Seat belt

Be a Good Passenger

Stay seated, keep your hands and head inside the car, and don't distract the driver. Help keep everyone safe!

Sit quietly
Keep seat belt on
Don't lean out windows
Don't unbuckle while moving

Bicycle Safety

Helmet Safety is #1!

How to Wear Your Helmet Correctly:

  • Helmet should sit level on your head
  • Straps should form a "V" under your ears
  • Only 1-2 fingers should fit under the chin strap
  • Helmet should not rock back and forth

Bike Riding Rules

Look Both Ways

Always stop and look left, right, then left again before crossing streets or driveways.

Ride on the Right

Always ride on the right side of the path or road, in the same direction as cars.

Use Hand Signals

Point where you're going and use hand signals to let others know your plans.

Be Visible

Wear bright colors and use lights when riding in the evening. Make sure people can see you!

Walking Safely

How to Cross the Street Safely

1

Stop at the Curb

Always stop completely before entering the street, even if you don't see any cars.

2

Look Left, Right, Left

Look left first, then right, then left again. Keep looking while you cross.

3

Listen for Cars

Use your ears too! Listen for car engines, horns, or other vehicle sounds.

4

Cross at Crosswalks

Always use crosswalks or intersections. Don't cross between parked cars.

5

Keep Looking

Continue to look and listen for vehicles while crossing the street.

Smart Walking Tips

Walk with Adults

Young children should always walk with a grown-up, especially near busy streets.

Stay on Sidewalks

Always use sidewalks when available. If there's no sidewalk, walk facing traffic.

Make Eye Contact

When crossing, try to make eye contact with drivers to make sure they see you.

Put Devices Away

Don't use phones or tablets while walking near roads. Pay attention to your surroundings.
